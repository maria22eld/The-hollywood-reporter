The Rise of Streaming Platforms and the Threat to Cinema

Published in 2026 • The Hoolywood Reporter
As streaming expands, the cinema experience faces an uncertain future.

The rapid rise of streaming platforms such as Netflix, Disney+, and many others highlights why we must actively support the cinema industry — to ensure that this immersive experience continues to exist.

An expanding industry — at a cost

Hundreds of streaming applications and websites have been launched in recent years, making it clear that this business is expanding rapidly. However, this growth raises serious concerns about the future of movie theaters and theatrical releases.

A worrying signal from the box office

Last week, the movie “Clear” was released in theaters. Statistics had predicted that one million tickets would be sold. In reality, only five hundred thousand tickets were purchased.

This gap cannot be ignored. If this trend continues, the cinema industry may soon lack the financial resources needed to produce ambitious films — movies that allow audiences to escape reality and discover entirely new worlds.

The irreplaceable cinema experience

Watching a movie in a theater offers an immersive experience that cannot be replicated at home. In a cinema, all our senses are engaged. We share emotions with strangers, react together, and feel part of a collective moment.

The smell of popcorn, the darkness of the room, the power of sound — all these elements bring back memories of watching films with friends, especially during our teenage years. It is an experience that should not disappear.

“Cinema is not just about watching a film — it is about living it together.”

These warnings should not be ignored. We must strengthen and support the cinema industry before it is too late. Cinema has given us some of our most powerful memories — and it deserves to be protected.
