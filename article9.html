The Films That Changed Cinema: From Citizen Kane to The Dark Knight

Published in 2026 • The Hoolywood Reporter
A few films don’t just succeed — they reshape cinema itself.

Throughout the history of cinema, certain films have stood out for their lasting impact on audiences and on the industry. These movies are remembered not only for their commercial success, but also for their artistic and technical achievements.

Classics that transformed storytelling

One of the most influential films ever made is The Godfather (1972), directed by Francis Ford Coppola. The movie is widely regarded as a masterpiece thanks to its strong storytelling, iconic performances, and realistic depiction of organized crime. It is still studied in film schools and continues to influence modern filmmakers.

Another major classic is Citizen Kane (1941). The film introduced innovative filming techniques and narrative structures that changed the way stories could be told on screen, becoming a reference point for generations of directors.

Global success and technical achievement

One of the most successful films in cinema history is Titanic (1997), directed by James Cameron. The movie tells the story of the tragic sinking of the Titanic while combining romance and historical drama. It became a worldwide phenomenon, broke box office records, and won multiple Academy Awards.

Its emotional impact, combined with impressive visual effects, made it one of the most memorable cinematic experiences ever created.

Modern blockbusters that became timeless

Other films such as Star Wars (1977) and The Dark Knight (2008) also played a major role in shaping modern cinema. According to many critics, these movies remain timeless examples of great filmmaking and continue to influence directors today.

From artistic revolutions to technical breakthroughs, these films show how cinema evolves — and how a few masterpieces can leave a permanent mark on the world.
