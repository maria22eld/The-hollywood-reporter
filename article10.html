The Greatest TV Shows of All Time — And Why They Still Matter

Published in 2026 • Written by El Abdellaoui Malak .
Great TV series have reshaped storytelling and cultural expectations.

Television has evolved dramatically over the years, and some series have redefined what storytelling on screen can achieve. The greatest TV shows of all time are not only popular — they are influential, shaping culture, audience expectations, and the television industry itself.

When television reached cinematic quality

Breaking Bad is often considered one of the greatest series ever made. Its powerful storytelling and deep character evolution transformed a simple crime story into a psychological drama. The show proved that television could reach the same artistic level as cinema.

Similarly, Game of Thrones changed television by bringing cinematic visuals, complex political narratives, and large-scale production to the small screen — even if its ending divided audiences.

Realism, society, and cultural impact

Other series such as The Sopranos and The Wire are praised for their realism and strong social commentary. They explored themes like power, crime, and corruption in a way that felt honest and thought-provoking.

Meanwhile, shows like Friends and Stranger Things became global cultural phenomena, influencing fashion, music, and popular culture across generations.

In my opinion, the greatest TV shows are those that go beyond entertainment. They tell meaningful stories, reflect society, and stay in people’s minds long after the final episode. These series prove that television is one of the most powerful storytelling mediums of our time.
